Sentiment Analysis of Amazon Product Reviews

Sentiment Analysis uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to transform unstructured textual data into structured data on user opinions. Reviews are then classified into positive, negative, or neutral to provide an understanding of the products. The process involves text preprocessing, feature extraction, and computational modeling to interpret sentiment patterns. Sentiment analysis supports informed decision-making and customer-focus.

Introduction

He had little to bite and to break, and once, when great scarcity fell on the land, he could no longer procure even daily bread. Now when he thought over this by night in his bed, and tossed about in his anxiety, he groaned and said to his wife: "What is to become of us? Data

In this project, I am using product review data provided by Amazon through the Amazon S3 service. While web scraping could be an effective method for gathering this data, it is essential to adhere to Amazon's terms of service, which prohibit scraping without prior permission. To comply with both ethical and legal standards, I have opted to use Amazon's publicly available data, ensuring that all data usage remains within the allowed guidelines. This approach also offers practical benefits, such as ensuring data consistency and maintaining reproducibility throughout the project.
